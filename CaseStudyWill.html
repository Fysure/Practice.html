Case Study

Problem Statement

Solution

There is not enough emphasis on the statistical implications of facial recoginition, as well as the settings of the camera used to identify different individuals.
Update face ID algorithms with frequent, diverse data and update camera quality.

"In general, one of the main reasons for bias might be the induction of unequally distributed classes in training data," meaning most inaccurate models do not use even amounts of data for lighter and darker skinned people.

Companies developing these facial recognition systems should use larger samples to more consistently recognize people.

Have government regulations on the camera settings so that it is universal in quality. This ensures companies will make an effort to equitize face ID algorithms, and will be able to update the quality of said camera frequently.
